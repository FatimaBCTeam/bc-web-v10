CASL Compliance

BC Team is committed to full compliance with Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL), S.C. 2010, c. 23, and its associated regulations. This section describes how we obtain consent, what commercial electronic messages (CEMs) we send, and how you can withdraw consent at any time.

What Is CASL?

CASL is a Canadian federal law that governs the sending of commercial electronic messages to Canadian recipients. A commercial electronic message (CEM) is any electronic message — including email — that encourages participation in a commercial activity, regardless of whether there is an expectation of profit. CASL requires that senders obtain express or implied consent before sending CEMs, identify themselves clearly in every message, and provide a simple unsubscribe mechanism in every CEM.

How BC Team Obtains Consent

BC Team relies on the following forms of consent for commercial communications:

  • Express consent — You have affirmatively opted in to receive communications from BC Team by checking a consent checkbox on a web form, subscribing to a newsletter, or otherwise clearly agreeing to receive our marketing communications. All express consent records include the date, time, source, and the specific consent language displayed to you at the time of opt-in.
  • Implied consent — existing business relationship. If you are an existing BC Team client or have engaged with us in a transaction within the past two (2) years, CASL permits us to send you commercial communications related to our services during that period without requiring separate express consent, unless you have unsubscribed.
  • Implied consent — conspicuous publication. If you have conspicuously published your business contact information (e.g., on a public website or business directory) and your message relates to your professional role, CASL permits contact in certain circumstances. BC Team uses this basis only for initial outreach and does not continue sending CEMs without obtaining express consent or establishing a business relationship.

BC Team does not purchase email lists or send unsolicited bulk commercial email. Every recipient of our marketing communications has either expressly opted in or falls within a recognized CASL implied consent category.

What We Send

BC Team may send the following types of commercial electronic messages to recipients who have provided consent:

  • BC tips, educational content, and insights about Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central
  • Information about BC Team products (RAP, DMS, FieldOps) and service offerings
  • Case studies, client results, and industry research
  • Invitations to webinars, demos, or educational events
  • Promotional offers related to BC Team products and services

Every commercial electronic message we send clearly identifies BC Team as the sender, includes our mailing address, and provides a simple unsubscribe mechanism.

How to Unsubscribe

You can withdraw your consent to receive commercial electronic messages from BC Team at any time, at no cost, using any of the following methods:

  • Unsubscribe link: Click the "Unsubscribe" link included at the bottom of every marketing email we send. Unsubscribe requests made via email link are processed within 10 business days, as required by CASL.
  • Email: Send an unsubscribe request directly to bc@bcteam1.com with "Unsubscribe" in the subject line. Include the email address you wish to remove.
  • Mail: Send a written unsubscribe request to our mailing address below.

Upon receipt of a valid unsubscribe request, BC Team will cease sending commercial electronic messages to the specified address within 10 business days, as required by CASL. Unsubscribing from marketing communications does not affect transactional messages related to active client engagements (e.g., invoices, project updates, support responses).
